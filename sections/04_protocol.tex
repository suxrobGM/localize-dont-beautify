\section{Evaluation Protocol}
\label{sec:protocol}

A surgical preview fails in two opposite ways. It can change too much of the wrong
thing---drifting identity or beautifying the whole face---or it can change too
little of the right thing, returning a near-copy that scores deceptively well on
similarity metrics. Our protocol measures both directions at once.

\subsection{Identity}

We compute the cosine similarity between ArcFace~\cite{deng2019arcface} embeddings
of the largest detected face in the input and in the edit, and adopt $0.6$ as the
acceptance floor. Two caveats govern its reading. First, a very high score can mean
the model simply did not perform the edit; identity is never interpreted without
the regional-change scores below. Second, as Agarwal and
Bhrany~\cite{agarwal2026envisage} point out, any identity score computed on a
composited output partially measures pixels that were copied from the input by
construction. We therefore treat identity on the prompt-only rung as the
\emph{model-level} measurement and identity on composited rungs as a
\emph{system-level} property, and we lean on the localization metric---which looks
inside and outside the mask separately---for the claims compositing actually makes.

\subsection{Regional change and localization}

After aligning the edit onto the input, we convert both to CIELAB and take the mean
$\Delta E_{76}$ color difference inside the procedure's target region
($\Delta E_{\mathrm{tgt}}$) and inside a disjoint off-target keep zone
($\Delta E_{\mathrm{off}}$, the upper-face region minus the target). At the
magnitudes involved the refinements of CIEDE2000~\cite{sharma2005ciede2000} do not
change the ordering, so we keep the simpler formula. The summary statistic is the
localization ratio
\[
  \mathrm{loc} = \frac{\Delta E_{\mathrm{tgt}}}
                      {\Delta E_{\mathrm{tgt}} + \Delta E_{\mathrm{off}}},
\]
read jointly with its parts: high $\Delta E_{\mathrm{tgt}}$ with near-zero
$\Delta E_{\mathrm{off}}$ is a confined surgical edit; low
$\Delta E_{\mathrm{tgt}}$ is the did-nothing trap regardless of the ratio; high
$\Delta E_{\mathrm{off}}$ is the beauty filter. Masked preservation scores exist in
general-domain editing benchmarks~\cite{qian2025giebench,alebench2024,
sheynin2024emuedit}; ours differs in being anchored to clinically meaningful facial
regions and paired with identity as a dual failure-mode detector. Because the
region masks are defined by the frontal mesh, the metric abstains (returns no
score) on non-frontal inputs rather than guessing.

\subsection{Ground-truth sanity check}

For faces with a real post-operative photograph we also report the ArcFace cosine
between the edit and that photograph. This is deliberately a soft sanity check, not
a ranking target: the post-op sessions differ in pose, expression, makeup, and
hair, and in pilot experiments geometric proxies such as the gonial angle flipped
sign under that noise. A preview that lands in the right neighborhood of the real
outcome should score moderately here; nothing stronger is claimed.

\subsection{Expert rubric (protocol only)}

We release a seven-axis rubric---identity preservation, photorealism, procedure
specificity, clinical plausibility, edit localization, consistency, and safety of
the expectation it sets---each scored 1--5, with clinical plausibility reserved for
a surgeon. The pre-registered pass bar for a procedure is a median clinical
plausibility of at least 4, identity cosine of at least $0.6$, and no axis median
below 3. No rubric scores were collected in this study; the automated metrics above
are the evidence presented, and the surgeon-scored study is the gating next step
(Sec.~\ref{sec:conclusion}).
