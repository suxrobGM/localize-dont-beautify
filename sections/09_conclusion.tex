\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

In this pilot, text instructions alone did not prevent hosted image editors from
changing a predefined facial keep zone. A landmark-derived mask, alignment, and
client-side feathered composite reduced that measured off-target change while
retaining similar target-region pixel change. This is useful engineering evidence:
off-mask pixel preservation can be enforced outside a black-box model. The tested
Qwen inpainting endpoint did not outperform that post-hoc control, but one endpoint
cannot support a general conclusion about masked inpainting.

The study does not validate cosmetic-surgery outcome prediction. The main analysis
contains \NumPrimaryFaces{} faces, one generated output per cell, no surgeon
ratings, limited profile support, and no metric of improvement over the original
image relative to postoperative anatomy. The next study should add repeated
generations, pose-robust whole-image localization, input-relative postoperative
metrics, multiple blinded surgeons with inter-rater agreement, a larger
demographically characterized cohort, documented image-use authorization, and
immutable or self-hosted model baselines. Until then, client-side compositing
should be understood as a control for off-target pixel changes, not as evidence
that the resulting preview is clinically accurate or safe for patient counseling.
