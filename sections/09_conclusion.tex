\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Off-the-shelf hosted image editors cannot yet be trusted to confine a surgical
edit on their own---prompt-only control leaks half the change off-target, and the
one mask-accepting endpoint we tested under-edits---but they do not need to be.
A landmark-derived mask, an alignment step, and a feathered composite on the
client turn the same endpoints into region-confined preview editors with
localization near 1 and identity comfortably above the acceptance floor, at a
median cost of \$\MedCost{} per image. That combination, not any single model, is
the finding: the capability gap is in control, and control can live outside the
model. The gating next step is clinical rather than technical---collecting
surgeon scores under the released rubric---alongside inpaint-capable endpoints
that actually edit, pose-robust masking for profile views, and a demographically
stratified test set.
